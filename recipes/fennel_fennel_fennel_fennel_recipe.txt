Creating a dish centered around fennel can be a delightful way to highlight this versatile and aromatic vegetable. Here's a recipe idea that celebrates fennel in various forms:

### Fennel Quartet Salad

#### Ingredients:
- **4 bulbs of fresh fennel**
- **1 tablespoon olive oil**
- **Juice of 1 lemon**
- **Salt and pepper, to taste**
- **1 tablespoon fennel seeds**
- **1 bunch fresh dill**
- **Parmesan cheese, shaved (optional)**
- **1 teaspoon honey (or maple syrup)**

#### Instructions:
1. **Prepare the Fennel:**
   - Trim the fennel bulbs, removing the stalks and fronds (reserve some fronds for garnish). Cut the bulbs in half and slice thinly using a mandolin or sharp knife.

2. **Roast the Fennel:**
   - Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
   - Take one bulb of fennel, chop it into wedges, and drizzle with olive oil, salt, and pepper. Place in a single layer on a baking sheet.
   - Roast in the oven for about 20-25 minutes until the fennel is tender and slightly caramelized.

3. **Make Pickled Fennel:**
   - Take another bulb, slice thinly, and place in a bowl.
   - Add juice of 1 lemon and a pinch of salt. Let it sit for at least 15 minutes to lightly pickle and soften.

4. **Toast the Fennel Seeds:**
   - In a small skillet, toast the fennel seeds over medium heat until fragrant, about 2 minutes. Set aside.

5. **Assemble the Salad:**
   - In a large bowl, combine the remaining raw fennel slices with roasted and pickled fennel.
   - Toss with olive oil, a drizzle of honey, lemon juice, salt, and pepper to taste.
   - Add toasted fennel seeds and fresh dill.
   - Optionally, top with shaved Parmesan cheese for extra flavor.

6. **Garnish and Serve:**
   - Garnish with some of the reserved fennel fronds.
   - Serve the salad as a refreshing side dish or light main course.

This Fennel Quartet Salad brings out different textures and flavors of fennel, showcasing its unique blend of sweetness and anise-like aroma. Enjoy!
